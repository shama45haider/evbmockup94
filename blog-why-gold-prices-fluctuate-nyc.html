Why Gold Prices Fluctuate (And When to Sell in NYC)

If you've been paying attention to the news lately, you've probably heard that gold is sitting near all-time highs. But if you check the market day by day, you'll notice the price never stays still. It jumps up, dips down, and constantly shifts. So, what actually causes gold prices to fluctuate, and more importantly—when is the right time to sell?

The Forces Behind the Gold Market

Gold isn't just a shiny metal; it's a global currency and a safe-haven asset. When we buy and sell gold in NYC, we are looking directly at the live spot price, which is influenced by a few major factors:

  • Inflation and Currency Values: When the dollar weakens or inflation rises, investors flock to gold to protect their wealth. This drives the price up.
  • Global Uncertainty: Political tensions, market crashes, and global events make people nervous. In uncertain times, gold is the ultimate safety net.
  • Supply and Demand: Like any commodity, if jewelry demand spikes or mining slows down, the price reacts accordingly.
