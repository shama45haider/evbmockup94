How It Works

Sell in One Visit

Most folks are in and out in 20–30 minutes. We authenticate everything in front of you, walk you through the numbers, and you leave with cash the same day if you're happy with the offer. One pair or a collection — same process, same respect for your kicks.

  1. Bring your sneakers and a valid ID

    Come to 39 Avenue A with the kicks you want to sell. Sneakers in any condition — beat, deadstock, worn once. Bring original box and paperwork if you have it; we'll take them into account. Text photos to 917-608-8939 first if you've got a big collection and want to make sure we have time.

  2. We authenticate and assess condition

    We'll check the sneakers for authenticity — tags, stitching, materials, manufacturing details. We look at sole condition, box condition (if you have it), and any wear or damage. You'll see everything we're looking at; we explain the assessment as we go.

  3. We walk you through the offer — zero pressure

    We'll explain the brand, condition grade, current market demand, and what we're willing to pay. No hard sell, no rushing. Shop around if you want — there's no charge for the look-see and no obligation if you need time to think.

  4. Say yes and get paid same day

    If you're happy, you walk out with cash that day. Big collections? We can work with check payment if you prefer — just ask at the counter.

Jordans

We Buy & Sell Jordans in NYC

Air Jordan 1s, 3s, 11s, Retro Highs and Lows, OG colorways, rare releases. Every pair gets authenticated, graded, and priced fairly at the counter.

Whether it's a crispy Bred 1, a '90s Concord, or a newer collaboration, we know the details. We check materials, tags, stitching, and construction. Worn pairs? Deadstock? Original box? We factor all of it in. Bring what you have and we'll explain the offer before you decide anything.

What types of Jordans do you buy?

Pretty much all of them. Air Jordan 1s through 37s, retro releases, OG colorways, limited collabs, and special editions. Highs, Lows, Mids — condition matters but we buy across the board.

Do you buy worn or beat Jordans?

Yes. Condition affects the price but we buy them. Creased toe box, creased midsole, worn leather — none of that kills the offer. We grade it honestly and price accordingly.

How do you authenticate Jordans?

We look at materials, stitching patterns, tag details, midsole construction, toe box shape, and overall quality. Every Jordan has tells; we know what to look for. Fakes are obvious when you know what you're doing.

Does the original box add value?

Yes, especially for deadstock or lightly worn pairs. An OG box in good condition can add to the offer. Box-less pairs are still worth money when the shoes are right.

What about OG pairs and retro releases?

OGs can be valuable depending on condition and colorway. Retro releases have different values than OG pairs. We know the difference and price them separately. Bring them both if you have them.

Do you buy collaborative Jordans?

All the time. Travis Scott, Dior, Union, Travis Scott, Sacai — collabs get special attention and can command higher prices when authentic and in decent shape.

How is a Jordan offer calculated?

Model, colorway, condition, market demand, and whether the box is included. We check current resale prices for the same pair and work from there. We'll explain the math at the counter.

Can I sell multiple pairs at once?

Absolutely. We'll authenticate and price each pair individually. Whether you've got one or ten, we go through them the same way — careful, honest, and fair.

Do you buy Size exclusives?

Yes. Some pairs are size-specific and that affects value. Smaller and larger sizes can be harder to move so the price reflects that, but we still buy them.

Should I clean my Jordans before selling?

Light cleaning is fine but don't use harsh products or wire brushes. A gentle wipe is okay. We're looking at the shoe itself not how shiny you make it. Leave the authentication for us.

Nike

We Buy & Sell Nike Sneakers in NYC

Nike SB Dunks, Air Force 1s, React, Blazers, special releases and rare Nike collaborations. We authenticate and grade every pair.

From skate shoes to basketball to collaborations, Nike makes collector-friendly sneakers. Dunks in particular have a huge secondary market and we stay current on values. Whether it's a classic SB or a Sacai collab, we know what to look for in terms of authenticity, condition, and current demand.

What types of Nike sneakers do you buy?

SB Dunks (lows, highs, mids), Air Force 1s, React, Blazers, Cortez, various collaborations. If it has a Swoosh and there's a market for it, we're interested.

Do you specialize in SB Dunks?

We buy plenty of them. Dunks have strong resale value and consistent demand. Vintage SBs, recent releases, rare collabs — we know the market and price them fairly.

How do you authenticate Nike sneakers?

Stitching patterns, Swoosh shape, tag details, midsole construction, materials. Nike has consistent quality standards and fakes usually fail on one or more of these points.

Do you buy Nike collaboration sneakers?

Yes. Sacai, Stranger Things, Travis, Supreme — collabs often have higher value than general release. We price them accordingly when they're legit.

What about vintage Nike and older releases?

We buy them when we can authenticate them. Older construction details are different; we know what to look for. Deadstock vintage can be valuable but condition tells all.

Do you buy Air Force 1s?

Yes, though standard AF1s have lower resale unless they're special editions or collab versions. We'll give you a fair number if you bring them in.

How do I know if my Nike sneakers are worth selling?

Bring them in for a free quote. Limited releases, special editions, collabs, and pristine condition pieces are usually worth more. We'll let you know what the market pays.

What condition do Nike sneakers need to be in?

Any condition works. Deadstock, worn once, creased, beaten — all of it gets graded and priced. Lower condition = lower offer but you'll still get a number.

Can I sell a pair with a damaged box?

Sure. Box condition affects the offer but torn or missing boxes won't kill the deal. Shoes are what matter most; the box is secondary.

Do you buy women's and kids' sizes?

Yes. Smaller sizes sometimes have smaller resale audiences so price reflects that, but we absolutely buy them when they're authentic.

Yeezy

We Buy & Sell Yeezy in NYC

Yeezy Boosts, Foam Runners, Slides, and official Adidas collaborations. We authenticate everything and price current market values.

Yeezy sneakers have strong secondary-market demand and values fluctuate with news and availability. Boosts especially — we know the colorways, the manufacturing details, and what's valuable. Whether you're selling a collection or a single pair, we verify authenticity and grade condition fairly.

What types of Yeezy do you buy?

Yeezy Boosts (350, 500, 700, 750 V1 and V2), Foam Runners, Foam RNNR, Slides, QNTM, all official Adidas Yeezy collaborations.

Do you buy Yeezy Boosts of all generations?

Yes. V1 and V2 750s, 350 versions, 500s, 700s, 950s — each has different value depending on colorway and condition. We know the details on each.

How do you authenticate Yeezy sneakers?

Boost pattern, tag details, shoe shape, midsole construction, materials. Fakes usually have obvious tells in the Boost or tag area. We spot them quickly.

What about Yeezy Foam Runners?

We buy them. They're a different category from sneakers but have collector following. Condition, colorway, and current demand all matter. Values have fluctuated so bring them in for a quote.

Do you buy Yeezy Slides?

Yes. Slides have different resale value than Boosts or Runners but we buy them. Common colorways have lower value; limited colors and earlier releases can be worth more.

How do Yeezy prices compare to other sneakers?

Depends on the model and colorway. Some Boosts and limited Runners outprice Jordans; Slides are typically lower. We price each individually based on current market.

What condition does Yeezy need to be in?

Any. Deadstock Boosts in OG box fetch more but worn pairs still have value. Condition grades everything — you're not leaving empty-handed.

Do you take account of original receipts or provenance?

It's nice to have but not required. The shoe itself is what matters. We authenticate from the physical sneaker; documentation is a bonus if it's there.

Can I sell a Yeezy with no original box?

Absolutely. Box-less lowers the offer but doesn't kill it. The shoe is what counts; box is secondary value.

Should I clean my Yeezy before selling?

Don't use harsh chemicals. A light wipe is fine. We're assessing the shoe not its shine; leave aggressive cleaning alone.

Off-White

We Buy & Sell Off-White Sneakers in NYC

Off-White Nike collaborations, Air Jordans, rare drops, and special releases. We authenticate everything thoroughly.

Off-White commands premium prices when authentic. The details matter — the quotation marks, the stitching, the tag construction, how the shoelace label is placed. We know the tells and we authenticate carefully. Fakes are common; real pairs with good provenance are valuable.

What Off-White sneakers do you buy?

Off-White Nike collaborations (The Ten, recent drops), Off-White Air Jordans, and other official Virgil Abloh Adidas/Nike pieces. Fakes are everywhere so authentication is critical.

How do you authenticate Off-White?

Quotation mark placement and quality, stitching precision, tag details, hang tag accuracy, logo placement, overall construction. Fakes usually fail on multiple points. We check everything.

Do you buy Off-White from The Ten collection?

Yes. The Ten pairs have strong resale; authentication is non-negotiable because fakes are everywhere. Bring original receipt if you have it; we'll verify everything at the counter.

What about Off-White Air Jordans?

High value and high fake rate. We authenticate thoroughly — materials, Jumpman logo details, Off-White elements, overall construction. Bring whatever documentation you have.

Does Off-White with original receipt sell for more?

It can, especially if the receipt matches the shoe details. A receipt doesn't guarantee we'll buy a fake but it's helpful. The shoe itself must be legit regardless.

How do prices compare for Off-White vs regular Nike/Jordan?

Off-White commands premium when authentic. A Travis Scott collab or The Ten piece is worth significantly more than a standard Nike or Jordan. We price accordingly.

What condition does Off-White need to be in?

Deadstock is best but we buy worn pairs too. Condition grades the price but you'll get an offer when it's authentic.

Do you buy rare or discontinued Off-White pieces?

Yes. Older Off-White drops, limited releases, and pieces from before the brand wound down — all desirable when legit. Bring them in for an offer.

Can I sell a box-less Off-White?

It affects the offer but doesn't kill it. The shoe must be authentic either way and condition matters most after that.

How important is the hang tag?

It's a useful authentication checkpoint but not required. Some people lose hang tags. The shoe itself is what makes or breaks the sale.

Vintage

We Buy Vintage & Deadstock Sneakers in NYC

90s retro releases, original Nike and Jordan drops, deadstock pairs, and collector sneakers. We know vintage details and price accordingly.

Vintage sneakers have serious collectors and values can be high when the condition is right. A 1985 Jordan 1 Red, a deadstock 1995 Air Max, a pristine Nike dunk from the early 2000s — we authenticate and price fairly. Deadstock is premium; worn vintage is still valuable depending on rarity and condition.

What counts as vintage sneakers?

Generally 1980s-2000s releases, original Nike and Jordan drops, Air Max and Air Force 1 early versions, and any deadstock or well-preserved shoes from that era.

Do you buy deadstock vintage?

Yes — this is premium stock. Deadstock '90s Jordans, original Air Maxes in the box, unworn retro releases — we pay accordingly when we can authenticate them.

How do you authenticate vintage sneakers?

Tag details, stitching patterns, midsole technology, shoe shape, materials — all changed over time. We know the differences between eras and spot inconsistencies that suggest fakes or reproductions.

What about worn vintage sneakers?

Still valuable if they're rare and condition is acceptable. A worn OG '85 Chicago 1 has serious value. We grade condition and price accordingly.

Do you buy 1990s Nike and Jordan originals?

Absolutely. Early Air Jordans, '90s Dunks, vintage Air Force 1s, original Air Max — these are collector pieces. When authentic, they command real money.

How much does the original box matter for vintage?

A lot. OG box in good shape can significantly increase value. Box condition matters almost as much as shoe condition for premium vintage pieces.

What's the oldest sneaker you would buy?

We handle pairs from the 1980s forward. If it's a legitimate original and it's rare we'll take a look. Come with what you have and let us evaluate it.

Do vintage sneakers need to be in pristine condition?

Not necessarily. Deadstock should be pristine but vintage pairs can have wear. Rarity and authenticity matter more than perfection. We grade what we see.

How are vintage sneaker prices determined?

Rarity, condition, market demand, and completeness (shoes + box + paper). A rare pair in good condition with OG box commands more. We check current comparable sales and price fairly.

Can I sell a mixed collection of vintage and modern?

Of course. We price each pair individually. Vintage gets premium treatment; modern gets standard grading. One visit, all of it handled fairly.

Other Heat

We Buy Other Hype Sneakers in NYC

Hot sneaker culture expands beyond just Jordans and Nike. Collabs with designers and musicians, independent brands with cult followings, limited-edition drops from Stüssy to New Balance — we know the landscape and what's valuable. When you've got heat from lesser-known brands, bring it to us for a real offer.

What hype brands do you buy?

Travis Scott collabs, New Balance, Stüssy, Sacai, ASICS, Salomon, Reebok collaborations, Patta, Atmos, Dover Street Market — basically any brand with a collector following.

Do you buy Travis Scott sneakers?

Yes. Travis collabs have strong value — Jordan 1 Retro High OG "Reverse Mocha," Air Force 1 Cactus Jack, and others. We authenticate and price them fairly when you bring them in.

What about New Balance collaborations?

New Balance collabs with Kawhi Leonard, Salehe Bembury, Brain Dead, New Era — we buy them. Standard NBs have lower value but limited collabs are real money.

Do you buy Stüssy sneakers?

Yes — Stüssy has a strong collector base. Nike SB Dunks with Stüssy, vintage Stüssy pieces, collaborations — bring them in for an offer.

What about Sacai and designer collabs?

Sacai Nike pieces, Undercover collabs, designer collaborations — all collectable when legit. These command attention because availability is limited.

Do you buy ASICS and Salomon?

We buy collabs and limited releases. Standard ASICS or Salomon have lower resale; special editions and designer collabs have real value.

How do you know if a hype sneaker is worth something?

Limited drops, designer collaborations, and brands with collector followings are worth more. Bring it in and we'll check current market value. We're plugged into sneaker culture.

Can I sell sneakers from brands I've never heard of?

Bring them anyway. Independent brands and emerging labels sometimes have serious collector value. We'll let you know what the market pays if anything.

Do you buy limited-edition drops?

All day. That's where the heat is. SNKRS drops, in-store exclusives, pop-up releases, collaboration drops — we're always interested in limited stuff.

What if I'm not sure of the brand or release?

Bring it in. We'll identify it, check authenticity, and give you a number if there's value. No harm in asking — worst case we tell you it's not a buyer piece and you're out a quick trip.
