We're a real shop at 39 Avenue A — walk in with a single belt or a stack of wallets from your closet. We inspect authenticity and condition at the counter, explain the offer plainly, and pay cash the same day if you like the number. No consignment wait, no mail-in revised offers, no pressure.

How It Works

Sell in One Visit

Most folks are in and out in about 15–30 minutes. Checking what you've got is completely free — you only sell if the number feels right.

  1. Walk in (or text us first)

    Grab your accessories and a valid photo ID and come to 39 Avenue A. Selling multiple pieces or something rare? Text photos to 917-608-8939 ahead of time — it helps us set aside time.

  2. We inspect right in front of you

    We check stamps, serial numbers, hardware, leather condition, and brand-specific details. You'll see exactly what we're looking at before you decide anything.

  3. We walk you through the offer — zero pressure

    We'll explain how brand, model, condition, and current resale demand add up to your number. Shop around if you want — no charge for the look-see.

  4. Say yes and get paid today

    If you're happy with the offer, you walk out with cash the same day. High-value lots can sometimes be paid by check if you prefer.

Pricing

We look at brand, model, condition, current resale market demand, and whether you have original packaging. High-demand models — like a classic Gucci GG belt or a Louis Vuitton Zippy wallet — will fetch more than older or less-sought styles. We check live resale data, not arbitrary markdowns.

We can give a rough ballpark if you text photos to 917-608-8939, but a firm offer requires seeing the piece in person. Condition details — wear patterns, hardware, interior — can't always be judged from photos alone. The in-person appraisal is always free and takes just minutes.

The biggest factors that lower an offer: cracked or peeling leather, broken or tarnished hardware, missing buckles or clasps, faded monogram canvas, strong odors, heavy interior staining, or obvious repairs. Models that are out of style or lower in resale demand also affect pricing regardless of condition.
